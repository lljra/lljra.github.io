MADRE DE CACAO

Scientific Name

  • Gliricidia sepium

What is Madre de Cacao?

  • Madre de Cacao (Gliricidia sepium), or gliricidia, is a versatile, fast-growing, nitrogen-fixing tree of the family Fabaceae. It grows 2 to 15 meters tall with an open, rounded crown and seasonal bright pink to lilac flowers. The name means "mother of cocoa" due to its use as shade for cacao plantations.

History of Madre de Cacao

  • Native from Mexico to Colombia and Venezuela, Gliricidia sepium is common in Central America and has been introduced throughout the tropics. It provides shade for crops like coffee and cacao and is widely used in farming systems. Its genus name references its use as a traditional rodenticide.

Benefits of Madre de Cacao

  • Used in agroforestry, it acts as crop shade, living fences, and erosion control. As a nitrogen-fixing legume, it enriches soil and boosts crop yields without chemicals. Its leaves provide high-protein fodder, and its wood is durable fuelwood, charcoal, and termite-resistant posts.
