BANANA

Scientific Name

  • Musa acuminata

What is Banana?

  • The banana is the fruit of the genus Musa, family Musaceae, one of the world's most important fruit crops. Grown mostly in the tropics, bananas are valued worldwide for their flavor, nutritional value, and year-round availability.

History of Banana

  • Bananas were first domesticated in Southeast Asia and later spread worldwide through trade and exploration. By the 19th century, bananas had become a staple fruit in many countries.

Benefits of Banana

  • Bananas are rich in potassium, fiber, and essential vitamins such as B6 and C. They provide quick energy, support digestion, and contribute to heart and muscle health.
