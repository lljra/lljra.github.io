SNAKE PLANT

Scientific Name

  • Dracaena trifasciata

What is the Snake Plant?

  • The Snake Plant (Dracaena trifasciata) is a hardy flowering plant native to West and West-Central Africa. It is adapted to dry, tropical climates and is characterized by thick, upright, sword-shaped leaves with distinctive banded patterns. The plant can produce clusters of cream-colored flowers and small orange fruits, though flowering is rare indoors. Today, it is widely cultivated around the world as a popular houseplant.

History of the Snake Plant

  • The Snake Plant has undergone several taxonomic changes throughout history. Initially grouped with Aloe by Carl Linnaeus in 1753, it was later placed in the genus Sansevieria by botanist David Prain in 1903. Advances in modern genome sequencing led to its reclassification under the genus Dracaena in 2017. The official name, Dracaena trifasciata, was published in Mabberley's Plant-book by David John Mabberley.

Benefits of the Snake Plant

  • Snake Plants are ideal for indoor landscaping due to their low maintenance requirements. Studies suggest that indoor greenery helps reduce stress, improve concentration, and support cardiovascular health. Research has also identified compounds in the plant that may help slow cancer cell movement and potentially aid in treating conditions such as alopecia.
