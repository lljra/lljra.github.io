HEART OF JESUS

Scientific Name

  • Caladium bicolor

What is Heart of Jesus?

  • The Heart of Jesus is a stunning tropical perennial known for its thin, heart-shaped, and translucent leaves. Scientifically called Caladium bicolor, it is popular as both a garden plant and a houseplant. Its foliage is famous for vibrant combinations of pink, red, white, and green, often featuring contrasting veins and borders that resemble angel wings.

History of Heart of Jesus

  • The Heart of Jesus originated in the tropical rainforests of South and Central America, particularly Brazil. It was introduced to Europe in the late 1700s and became highly popular during the 19th century as a greenhouse plant. Today, it is cultivated worldwide, with major hybridization centers in Florida and Southeast Asia producing hundreds of colorful varieties.

Benefits of Heart of Jesus

  • In tropical and subtropical landscapes, this plant is commonly used as a shade-loving groundcover, adding bright color to areas where other plants struggle to grow. It enhances shaded gardens, porches, and indoor spaces, helps create a lush tropical atmosphere, and is often given symbolically to represent joy and heartfelt affection.
