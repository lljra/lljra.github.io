HEART OF JESUS

Scientific Name

  • Caladium bicolor

What is Heart of Jesus?

  • Heart of Jesus is a stunning tropical perennial known for its thin, heart-shaped, and translucent leaves. Its scientific name is Caladium bicolor, and it is popular as both a garden plant and a houseplant. The foliage is admired for its vibrant blends of pink, red, white, and green, often highlighted by striking veins and borders.

History of Heart of Jesus

  • Heart of Jesus originated in the tropical rainforests of South and Central America, particularly Brazil. It was introduced to Europe in the late 1700s and became highly popular during the 19th century as a greenhouse ornamental. Today, it is cultivated worldwide, with many hybrid varieties developed for their unique colors and patterns.

Benefits of Heart of Jesus

  • This plant is widely used as a shade-loving ornamental, adding vivid color to gardens, patios, and indoor spaces. It enhances aesthetic appeal, creates a tropical atmosphere, and is easy to maintain in warm and humid environments. It is also commonly given as a symbolic plant representing joy and heartfelt affection.
