MANGO

Scientific Name

  • Mangifera indica

What is Mango?

  • Mango refers both to the plant Mangifera indica and its fruit. It is an evergreen tree belonging to the family Anacardiaceae. Mangoes are highly prized tropical fruits known for their sweet, juicy flavor and are among the most widely cultivated fruits in the world.

History of Mango

  • Mangoes have deep roots in Indian history and spirituality. Buddha was gifted a mango grove for meditation. The name "mango" likely originated from the Malayalam word manna, which Portuguese traders adapted to manga in 1498. Due to seed transport challenges, mangoes spread slowly and only reached the Americas around the 1700s, first planted in Brazil, then the West Indies.

Benefits of Mango

  • Mangoes are rich in vitamins A, C, and D. Studies suggest they support immunity, digestion, and even weight management. However, due to their natural sugar content, people with blood sugar concerns should consume them in moderation.
