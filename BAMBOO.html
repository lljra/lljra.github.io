BAMBOO

Scientific Name

  • Bambusoideae

What is Bamboo?

  • Bamboo is a group of tall, tree-like grasses belonging to the family Poaceae. It includes over 115 genera and more than 1,400 species. These plants thrive in tropical, subtropical, and mild temperate zones, with the highest diversity found in East and Southeast Asia, as well as in Pacific and Indian Ocean islands.

History of Bamboo

  • For centuries, bamboo has been an essential part of daily life, especially in East and Southeast Asia. It has been used for food, shelter, tools, paper-making, musical instruments, and traditional medicine, and remains deeply rooted in Asian culture and sustainability practices.

Benefits of Bamboo

  • Bamboo is a fast-growing, eco-friendly plant used in construction, crafts, and furniture. Its young shoots are edible, its fibers are useful for paper and textiles, and it plays an important role in environmental conservation by absorbing large amounts of carbon dioxide.
