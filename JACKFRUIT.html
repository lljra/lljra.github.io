JACKFRUIT

Scientific Name

  • Artocarpus heterophyllus

What is Jackfruit?

  • Jackfruit is the world’s largest tree-borne fruit, with individual fruits weighing up to 40 kilograms. Known as “nangka” in Indonesian and Malay, it is a fast-growing, drought-tolerant tree that is both resilient and incredibly versatile. Each tree can produce 100–200 fruits annually. Beyond food, the jackfruit tree has uses in traditional medicine, cultural rituals, and even construction.

History of Jackfruit

  • Believed to be native to South and Southeast Asia, jackfruit has been cultivated since ancient times. Some scholars trace its origin to India’s Western Ghats, while others point to Malaysia due to its fruit diversity. Jackfruit was domesticated independently in both regions and later spread via trade routes to Africa, Brazil, Jamaica, and Hawaii. It is now grown globally in tropical regions and is celebrated in cuisines for its meat-like texture, especially as a vegan alternative. It is the national fruit of Sri Lanka and Bangladesh, and a state fruit in parts of India.

Benefits of Jackfruit

  • Jackfruit is packed with fiber, vitamins A, B, and C, and essential minerals like potassium, magnesium, calcium, iron, copper, and phosphorus. It supports digestion, boosts immunity, promotes heart health, and aids in red blood cell production. Traditional Ayurvedic medicine uses jackfruit to help regulate blood sugar, soothe skin issues, and improve digestion.
