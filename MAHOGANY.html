MAHOGANY

Scientific Name

  • Swietenia mahagoni (West Indian mahogany), Swietenia macrophylla (Big-leaf mahogany), Swietenia humilis

What is Mahogany?

  • Mahogany is one of the most valuable timbers in the world, often called a “luxury wood.” It is the national tree of Belize. Overharvesting has led to conservation concerns; some species (like Swietenia macrophylla) are listed under CITES Appendix II (restricted trade).

History of Mahogany

  • Mahogany's name originated from a mix of African and American nomenclature during the transatlantic slave trade. French botanist Adrien de Jussieu clarified that African and American mahogany are different genera, creating a taxonomic distinction that persists today.

Benefits of Mahogany

  • The wood is rich in natural oils with antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it highly prized in furniture, construction, and decorative woodworking.
