ATIS

Scientific Name

  • Annona squamosa

What is Atis?

  • Atis, also known as sugar apple or sweet sop, is a small tropical tree belonging to the Annonaceae family. It produces round, green fruits with a scaly surface and soft, creamy white flesh that has a very sweet flavor. The fruit contains several black seeds and is commonly eaten fresh or used in desserts and beverages.

History of Atis

  • Atis is believed to have originated in tropical America, particularly the West Indies. It was introduced to Southeast Asia by Spanish traders during the 16th century and adapted well to the Philippine climate, becoming a popular backyard fruit tree across the country.

Benefits of Atis

  • The atis fruit is rich in vitamin C, fiber, and antioxidants that help strengthen the immune system, improve digestion, and promote healthy skin. Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine to help relieve coughs and minor infections.
