PEPPER

Scientific Name

  • Capsicum annuum

What is Pepper?

  • Pepper is a herbaceous plant that produces fruits ranging from sweet bell peppers to spicy varieties such as chili peppers. It belongs to the Solanaceae (nightshade) family and is widely cultivated for culinary, medicinal, and ornamental purposes.

History of Pepper

  • Peppers are native to the Americas and were an essential part of Aztec and Mayan diets for thousands of years. They were introduced to Europe by Christopher Columbus in the late 15th century, after which they spread rapidly across the world due to their adaptability and flavor.

Benefits of Pepper

  • Peppers are rich in Vitamin C, antioxidants, and essential nutrients. Spicy varieties contain capsaicin, a compound known to boost metabolism, relieve pain, and provide anti-inflammatory benefits. Regular consumption supports immune health and overall wellness.
