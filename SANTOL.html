SANTOL

Scientific Name

  • Sandoricum koetjape

What is Santol?

  • Santol fruits vary in size and shape depending on the variety and are typically round or oval. Their leathery, wrinkled rind can be yellowish or brown with a reddish blush, and may have a light fuzzy coating. Inside, the fruit contains five segments of white, cotton-like pulp surrounding large inedible seeds. The taste ranges from sour to sweet, with peach, apple, and citrus-like flavors.

History of Santol

  • Native to Southeast Asia, Santol has been used since ancient times. It likely originated in Cambodia, Laos, and Vietnam and spread throughout tropical Asia. Once a wild-growing fruit, it was later cultivated and bred for improved varieties. It now grows in home gardens, forests, and riverbanks and is commonly sold in fresh markets during harvest season.

Benefits of Santol

  • Santol is rich in vitamin C, fiber, iron, calcium, and magnesium. It helps boost immunity, improve digestion, and support bone health. In traditional medicine, various parts of the tree are used to treat infections, skin conditions, and inflammation. However, caution is needed — the seeds are inedible and can cause intestinal blockages if swallowed.
