CORN PLANT

Scientific Name

  • Dracaena fragrans

What is the Corn Plant?

  • The Corn Plant is an evergreen shrub belonging to the family Asparagaceae. It is a popular ornamental plant known for its thick, woody trunks and rosettes of long, arching leaves that resemble maize foliage. Often used indoors to add a tropical touch.

History of the Corn Plant

  • Native to tropical Africa, the Corn Plant has been used as a decorative houseplant in Europe since the mid-1800s. Its species name, fragrans, refers to its fragrant white flowers. It thrived in Victorian homes due to its low-light tolerance and resilience.

Benefits of the Corn Plant

  • Valued for air-purifying qualities, it removes toxins like formaldehyde and benzene. It is low-maintenance and drought-tolerant, perfect for improving indoor air quality.
