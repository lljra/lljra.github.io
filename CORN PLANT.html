CORN PLANT

Scientific Name

  • Dracaena fragrans

What is the Corn Plant?

  • The Corn Plant is an evergreen shrub belonging to the family Asparagaceae. It is a popular ornamental plant known for its thick, woody trunks and rosettes of long, arching leaves that resemble the foliage of maize. While it can grow quite tall in the wild, it is a slow-growing plant often used to add a tropical touch to indoor spaces.

History of the Corn Plant

  • Native to tropical Africa, the Corn Plant has been used as a decorative houseplant in Europe since the mid-1800s. Its species name, fragrans, refers to its clusters of white flowers that produce a strong, sweet scent at night. Historically, it was favored in Victorian homes because of its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and withstand neglect.

Benefits of the Corn Plant

  • This plant is highly valued for its air-purifying qualities. Studies, including those by NASA, show it effectively removes indoor toxins like formaldehyde and benzene. It is also exceptionally low-maintenance and drought-tolerant, making it a perfect choice for improving air quality in homes and offices.
